# Retention Strategy

## Objective

The objective of this strategy is to improve customer retention by targeting customer groups based on their RFM profile and behavioural signals such as support interactions, return behaviour, web activity, campaign engagement, and loyalty participation.

---

## Segment 1: Champions

### Characteristics

* Recent purchasers
* High purchase frequency
* High spending customers
* Strong engagement

### Retention Action

* VIP rewards
* Early access to new products
* Exclusive loyalty benefits

### Expected Business Value

Champions represent the most valuable customer segment and contribute significantly to revenue growth.

---

## Segment 2: Loyal Customers

### Characteristics

* Consistent purchasing behaviour
* Strong engagement history
* Stable customer relationship

### Retention Action

* Loyalty program upgrades
* Personalized product recommendations
* Referral incentives

### Expected Business Value

Strengthening loyalty can increase customer lifetime value and advocacy.

---

## Segment 3: Dormant Customers

### Characteristics

* Long time since last purchase
* Reduced engagement activity
* Declining customer relationship

### Retention Action

* Reactivation campaigns
* Limited-time offers
* Reminder emails and notifications

### Expected Business Value

Recovering dormant customers is often more cost-effective than acquiring new customers.

---

## Segment 4: Discount Sensitive Customers

### Characteristics

* High dependency on discounts
* Responsive to promotional offers
* Moderate spending behaviour

### Retention Action

* Personalized discount campaigns
* Coupon-based engagement
* Bundle offers

### Expected Business Value

Maintains purchase activity while encouraging repeat purchases.

---

## Segment 5: High Value But Unhappy Customers

### Characteristics

* High spending customers
* Support complaints or negative sentiment
* Elevated churn risk despite strong revenue contribution

### Retention Action

* Dedicated customer support outreach
* Priority issue resolution
* Personalized retention offers

### Expected Business Value

Retaining even a small number of these customers can generate substantial revenue impact.

---

## Campaign Budget Prioritization

Assume a retention budget of $10,000.

### Priority Order

1. High Value But Unhappy Customers
2. Dormant Customers
3. Loyal Customers
4. Discount Sensitive Customers
5. Champions

### Justification

High Value But Unhappy customers should receive the highest priority because they contribute significant revenue while displaying dissatisfaction signals.

Dormant customers represent recoverable revenue opportunities.

Champions already demonstrate strong engagement and therefore require relatively lower retention investment.

---

## Conclusion

Different customer segments require different retention approaches. A personalized retention strategy is expected to improve customer engagement, reduce churn risk, and maximize the return on retention spending.
